About Me

Most product managers come from engineering or business. I come from the fields of Tamil Nadu — and that's my biggest advantage.

During my B.Sc. (Hons) in Agriculture from Tamil Nadu Agricultural University, I didn't just study crops — I studied systems. I learned how soil, weather, nutrients, and human behavior interact to produce (or fail to produce) outcomes. That's product thinking in its rawest form.

Research · Agriculture · Data Analysis
"Effect of Bio-Stimulants on Soil Properties & Crop Yield of Greens"

The Problem

Conventional fertilizer trials lacked structured analysis on how bio-stimulants interact with integrated nutrient management to affect both soil physicochemistry and crop yield — leaving a critical evidence gap for sustainable farming decisions.

My Role

End-to-end research ownership: experimental design, field-based data collection, long-term fertilizer trial analysis, nutrient balance modeling, and authoring a structured assessment report.

Approach

Applied scientific methodology to isolate the effect of bio-stimulant products on soil pH, organic carbon, and crop yield metrics. Used long-term trial datasets and nutrient balance analysis to draw statistically-grounded conclusions.

Product Lens

Defined a research problem statement, structured a multi-variable experiment (akin to A/B testing), generated actionable insights from messy field data, and packaged findings for non-expert stakeholders — core PM skills in disguise.

🌱

Demonstrated measurable improvement in soil health metrics and yield outcomes with bio-stimulant integration — contributing usable data to sustainable agriculture practice guidance.

Field Research · User Interviews · Reporting
Farm Input Management Gap Analysis — Amrit Internship Program

The Problem

Agricultural input companies lacked structured data on how farmers in the region actually used fertilizers and pesticides — making it impossible to design relevant products, pricing, or outreach strategies.

My Role

Primary researcher: designed interview methodology, conducted structured interviews with 100 farmers, led large-scale field data collection, and synthesized findings into an executive-level assessment report.

Approach

Employed qualitative + quantitative research: farmer interviews for depth, large-scale surveys for breadth. Identified patterns in fertilizer usage behavior, knowledge gaps, and productivity barriers.

Product Lens

This was discovery research at scale — equivalent to user interviews and survey analysis in product development. Delivered a structured report highlighting gaps, trends, and opportunities (think: PRD-style output from raw research).

📊

Delivered a structured assessment report across 100 data points — surfacing actionable gaps in farm input management that could directly inform product and outreach strategy for agri-input stakeholders.
